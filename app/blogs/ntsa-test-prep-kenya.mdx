The NTSA is the gatekeeper. The National Transport and Safety Authority writes the highway code, issues the licences, runs the tests, and runs the [TIMS portal](https://tims.ntsa.go.ke) every learner driver hits in their first week. The NTSA test is the gate every Kenyan driver passes through. This page is about how to pass it without paying twice.

The short version: drill the Kenyan road signs cold, sit timed mock papers daily, learn the right-of-way rules until they are automatic. Theory is the bit most people underestimate, and it is the bit that fails them.

## What the test actually looks like

Two parts, sat one after the other.

**Theory.** Multiple-choice paper at an NTSA centre. Covers road signs, road markings, right of way, junctions, hazards and basic mechanical knowledge. Pass mark is ~70%. <Link to="/practice?mode=test">Practise the shape</Link>.

**Practical.** Yard work (reverse-into-bay, three-point-turn, parking) then a road drive with an NTSA examiner. Yard is where most candidates fall.

## What you have to know · six topic blocks

The NTSA theory paper draws from six topic blocks. DriveRush covers each — heavier on the topics that most often catch candidates out.

1. **Road signs & markings — largest section.** Warning, regulatory, mandatory and information signs, traffic-light signals, road markings. The category that decides most pass/fails. Reference: <Link to="/road-signs">all road signs</Link>.
2. **Right of way — junction-heavy.** Who goes first at unmarked junctions, signalled junctions, roundabouts and zebra crossings. Pure logic — drill it. Practice: <Link to="/practice?mode=challenge">scenarios</Link>.
3. **Highway code — rules section.** Speed limits, lane discipline, overtaking, parking, pedestrians, cyclists, boda bodas. The written rules of the Kenyan road. <Link to="/practice">Quick practice</Link>.
4. **Hazard perception — scenario-based.** Spot the developing hazard. The NTSA paper folds this into multiple-choice questions; the practical takes it on the road.
5. **Mechanical knowledge — smaller section.** Tyres, brakes, lights, oil, basic vehicle checks. Don't skip it — the questions are easy points for anyone who has read them once.
6. **Documents & licences — sometimes overlooked.** Insurance, NTSA inspection sticker, PDL, Smart DL renewals. A handful of test questions; the kind of thing roadside police actually check. [Smart DL guide](/blogs/smart-dl-replace-renew-lost).

## NTSA fees in 2026

Paid through TIMS with M-Pesa. Separate from any driving-school fees.

- **PDL · Class B:** KES 600
- **Theory test:** KES 1,000
- **Practical test:** KES 1,000
- **Smart DL card:** KES 3,000
- **Driving school theory (optional):** KES 3,000–5,000
- **Driving school practical:** KES 6,000–15,000

## The TIMS flow, in order

The walkthrough lives in its own piece: [how to apply for an NTSA driving licence in Kenya in 2026](/blogs/ntsa-driving-licence-kenya-2026). The short version:

1. **Open a TIMS account.** Use your real name as it appears on your ID. Verify the phone number you actually use.
2. **Apply for the PDL.** Choose your class (Class B for cars unless you know otherwise). Pay through M-Pesa.
3. **Book the theory test.** Pick the closest centre with the soonest slot. Most centres get rebooked within 48 hours.
4. **Sit the theory test, then the practical.** Yard then road. Most candidates fall on the yard.
5. **Pay the Smart DL fee on TIMS.** Once both tests are signed off. Pay from the wrong page and the receipt does not bind to your account.
6. **Collect the card.** Walk in with your ID, walk out with a Smart DL.
