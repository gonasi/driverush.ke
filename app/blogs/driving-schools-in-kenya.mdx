There are thousands of driving schools in Kenya. Some are excellent, most are fine, a handful are scams that print certificates and hope nobody notices. This is the short version of how to tell them apart, what the package should cost, and where to keep your wallet shut.

The single highest-leverage decision is one most learners get wrong: **whether to pay a school for theory at all**. The NTSA does not care where you learn theory. It cares whether you can pass the test. Self-study online, pay the school only for the wheel — your bill drops by KES 4,000–7,000, your pass rate does not.

## Online theory + in-person practical

A driving school in Kenya is mandatory for one reason: the NTSA needs a registered school to certify your practical hours before the test. Everything else is optional. Theory can be self-taught from your phone, on the matatu, between meetings. Practical cannot.

The schools that bundle "theory + practical" lean hard on the bundle because their margins are in the theory hours. A classroom can hold 30 students; a yard car can hold one student per hour.

## Six questions to ask before you pay

Good schools answer all six clearly. Skip the ones that don't.

1. **Are you NTSA-registered?** Every legitimate driving school in Kenya has an NTSA certificate on the wall. If they hesitate or wave it off, walk out. Hours from an unregistered school cannot certify you for the test.
2. **What does the package include?** Get a written breakdown: theory hours, practical hours, dual-control car use, mock test, NTSA test booking fee. Ask which items are extra. The cheapest sticker price often has the most exclusions.
3. **How many practical hours do I actually get behind the wheel?** An hour booked is not an hour driving. Ask whether you'll share the car with other students per session and how much pure wheel-time you get. Below 15 driving hours total, fail rates climb sharply.
4. **Who are the instructors and are they classified?** NTSA classifies driving instructors. Senior instructors charge more — they're also the ones who notice the bad habits. A school that won't name its instructor team is a school you don't want.
5. **Do you book the NTSA test for me?** Most do. Get the TIMS receipt or booking confirmation in your name, not the school's, before the test date. Schools that hold receipts on your behalf are a common source of delays.
6. **What happens if I fail the practical?** Reasonable schools include one retake or charge a known retake fee. The shady ones quietly upsell another full package. Get the policy in writing.

## What it should cost, in 2026

Prices vary by school, county and class. These are the bands you should see. Push back on quotes that sit far above the upper bound without a clear reason.

- **Class B theory only:** KES 0–5,000. Free with DriveRush; KES 3–5k if bought from a school. Don't overpay for theory.
- **Class B practical:** KES 6,000–15,000. Where the real money goes. 15–25 instructor hours plus dual-control car use. Push for more wheel-time, not a fancier brochure.
- **Class B full package:** KES 8,000–20,000. Theory + practical + booked NTSA test. Verify what's included in writing.
- **NTSA + Smart DL fees:** KES 3,000–4,000. Paid separately on TIMS. Theory test, practical test, Smart DL card. Same for every school.
- **Class A (motorbike):** KES 5,000–8,000. Shorter course, smaller fleet.
- **Class D (PSV):** KES 15,000–30,000. Longest course, strictest standards.

## Driving schools by region

DriveRush is online so it works everywhere there's a phone signal. Practical hours need a local NTSA-registered school. The cities with the most options:

- **Nairobi.** Most schools, most test centres, most options. AA, Petanns and Better Brakes have multiple branches.
- **Mombasa.** Coastal capital. AA, Glory and Mombasa Driving School lead.
- **Kisumu.** Lakeside hub. AA and Sunset cover most of the city.
- **Nakuru.** Active driving-school market thanks to commercial transport.
- **Eldoret.** Petanns, AA and a handful of local schools.
- **Thika & Kiambu.** Growing footprint, often cheaper than central Nairobi for the same content.

## The best-known schools, briefly

These are well-known, NTSA-registered nationwide chains. Reputation varies by branch, not by brand — always ask to see the certificate at the specific location you'll attend.

- **AA Kenya.** Oldest and largest. Predictable, slightly above market price.
- **Petanns Driving School.** Strong Nairobi/Eldoret presence. Mid-market.
- **Glory Driving School.** Coast-strong. Mid-market.
- **Better Brakes.** Nairobi-focused. Solid mid-market.
- **Sunset Driving School.** Western Kenya. Mid-market.
- **Eagle Driving School.** Multi-region. Mid-market.

There are hundreds of independent schools too. Many are excellent and cheaper than the chains. The selection criteria above matter more than the brand on the gate.
